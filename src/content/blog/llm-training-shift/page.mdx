## Why the training mix changed

Early large language models were mostly characterized by one dominant signal: a broad pretraining corpus scraped from text on the open web. That corpus still matters, but the models readers use today are shaped by additional supervision and feedback loops that arrive after pretraining.

Instruction and supervised examples teach format-following behavior. Preference comparisons steer style and safety. Verifiable reinforcement-learning tasks reward outcomes that can be checked automatically. Synthetic and model-generated traces expand coverage when human labels are scarce. On-policy distillation and self-distillation recycle the model's own outputs back into training.

Prose can list these signals, but a stacked timeline makes it easier to compare how their relative presence grows across eras without implying false precision.
