# Quality Documents Standards

Compact, reviewer-checkable quality rules for canonical docs pages. These
standards complement [writing standards](./writing-standards.md) and
[documentation template](./documentation-template.md).

## What counts as high quality

A high-quality reference page:

1. Opens with one folded `openingSummary` that states the reader problem and the
   page thesis in plain language (not separate problem/solution lead lines).
2. Uses the fewest words needed for accuracy — compact, minimalist, and free of
   filler.
3. Reads clearly for a technical layperson: short sentences, concrete nouns,
   active voice.
4. Stays on topic without tangents or undocumented prerequisites.
5. Backs technical claims with citations and links to related registry-backed
   pages instead of hand-maintained duplicate lists.
6. Avoids AI tone patterns, phase or factory process language, and
   reader-shortcut callouts in baseline templates.

## Reviewer checklist

Before approving a canonical docs page:

1. One folded summary at the top; no duplicate title chrome.
2. No phase, process, or meta language in customer-facing message files.
3. Math sections carry symbol-only definitions directly under equations.
4. Graphs, tables, and captions follow [graphing standards](./graphing-standards.md).
5. Narrative sections stay scannable; each paragraph advances one idea.

For the full writing contract, see [writing standards](./writing-standards.md).
