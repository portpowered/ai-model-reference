# Docs Quality Standards

Compact, reviewer-checkable quality rules for canonical docs pages. These
standards complement
[docs writing standards](./docs-writing-standards.md) and
[documentation template](../../../docs/documentation-template.md).

## Usage

Use this checklist as the concise acceptance pass for canonical docs writing.
The full writing contract lives in
[docs writing standards](./docs-writing-standards.md).

## What counts as high quality

A high-quality reference page:

1. Opens with one folded `openingSummary` that states the reader problem and
   the page thesis in plain language.
2. Uses the fewest words needed for accuracy and stays free of filler.
3. Reads clearly for a technical layperson with short sentences, concrete
   nouns, and active voice.
4. Makes sense in isolation before it narrows into a specific architecture,
   subsystem, or historical example.
5. States the reader payoff directly: why the concept matters, what pressure it
   addresses, or what decision it helps with.
6. Introduces full names before acronyms so a first-time reader is not asked to
   decode local shorthand.
7. Stays on topic without tangents, undocumented prerequisites, or repeated
   restatements of the same point.
8. Backs technical claims with citations and links to related registry-backed
   pages instead of hand-maintained duplicate lists.
9. Avoids AI tone patterns, phase or factory process language, and
   reader-shortcut callouts in baseline templates.

## Reviewer checklist

Before approving a canonical docs page:

1. One folded summary appears at the top with no duplicate title chrome.
2. The page works for a first-time reader without requiring adjacent pages.
3. The first sections explain both the concept and its value in plain language.
4. Titles and first mentions expand full names before acronyms or shorthand.
5. Customer-facing message files contain no phase, process, or meta language.
6. Math sections carry symbol-only definitions directly under equations.
7. Graphs, tables, and captions follow
   [graphing standards](../../../docs/graphing-standards.md).
8. Narrative sections stay scannable, each paragraph advances one idea, and
   each section contributes something new.
